Steven Paul Jobs

The man of vision and innovation

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs:

Early Career and Co-founding Apple:

  • Born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, USA.
  • Co-founded Apple Computer (now Apple Inc.) with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne in 1976.
  • Introduced the Apple I computer in 1976, and later, the Apple II in 1977, which became a commercial success.
  • In 1985, Jobs was ousted from Apple after a power struggle with the company's board of directors.

NeXT and Pixar:

  • Founded NeXT Computer in 1985, focused on creating high-end workstations and software development tools.
  • Acquired the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm and rebranded it as Pixar Animation Studios in 1986.
  • Pixar went on to create successful animated films like "Toy Story," "Finding Nemo," and "The Incredibles."

Return to Apple and Success with Innovative Products:

  • Apple acquired NeXT in 1997, and Jobs returned to Apple as its interim CEO.
  • Introduced the iMac in 1998, which revitalized Apple's product line and brought the company back into profitability.
  • Continued Apple's successful product launches, including the iPod (2001), iTunes (2003), iPhone (2007), and iPad (2010).
  • Under his leadership, Apple became one of the most valuable and influential companies globally.

Keynotes and Presentations:

  • Known for his charismatic presentations and famous "one more thing" surprises during Apple keynote events.

Health Issues and Departure from CEO Role:

  • Diagnosed with a rare form of pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor in 2003.
  • Resigned as CEO in August 2011 due to his health issues but continued as the chairman.

Passing and Legacy:

  • Passed away on October 5, 2011, at the age of 56, leaving a lasting impact on the tech and business world.
  • Remembered for his contributions to the development of personal computing, mobile technology, and entertainment, and for his vision and passion for design and user experience.
